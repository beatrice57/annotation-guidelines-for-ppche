Known issues

    Sentence token IDs
    Due to changes in sentence tokenization, the running ID numbers in the examples in the documentation no longer always match the current ID numbers. If necessary, the examples can be traced back to their source via the page numbers in the IDs, which are stable.

    ACCORDING TO and the like
    ACCORDING (TO), CONCERNING, REGARDING, and TOUCHING in their quasi-prepositional uses are not annotated alike. ACCORDING (TO) and REGARDING are tagged VAG and lemmatized as verbs, whereas CONCERNING and TOUCHING are tagged P and lemmatized as prepositions.

    ALL
    There likely remain inconsistencies in the annotation of ALL in its floated quantifier vs. intensifier use, both within PPCME2 and between PPCME2 and the later corpora.

    AS X SAID
    The -PRN dash tag on AS X SAID and similar parenthetical expressions is likely appended inconsistently, especially in the later corpora.

    Backwards gapping
    Backwards gapping may be mistagged as ordinary gapping.

    Departicipial adjectives
    Departicipial adjectives (INTERESTED, INTERESTING) in predicate position may be mistagged as participles (VAG, VAN), especially in the PPCME2.

    Comparative clauses with elision
    In comparative clauses with elision, brackets around elided subordinate clauses are likely to be missing.

    Direct speech
    Direct speech fragments may be labeled FRAG rather than QTP.

    Infinitival adjuncts
    Infinitival adjuncts (IP-INF-ADT) may be missing the -ADT tag.

    Measure phrases
    In searches for measure phrases (NP-MSR), it is wise to include QP, and vice versa.

    Proper nouns
    Many inconsistencies and outright errors likely remain in connection with proper nouns (NPR).

    The guidelines for proper nouns of the form THE N OF NP (THE WAR OF THE ROSES) have the counterintuitive result that none of the nouns is tagged NPR.

    Pseudo-imperatives
    Pseudo-imperatives are not annotated in the PCEEC.

    Purpose infinitives
    Purpose infinitives (IP-INF-PRP) may be missing the -PRP dash tag, particularly in connection with GO and SEND.

    Reduced relative clauses
    In searches for reduced relative clauses (RRC), it is wise to consider including ordinary participial clauses (IP-PPL), and vice versa.

    Right node raising
    Instances of right node raising may be missing the index required by the guidelines, particularly in the statutes.

    Secondary predicates
    Secondary predicates (ADJP-SPR, NP-SPR) may be mistagged as simple predicates (marked by -PRD) of small clauses (IP-SMC), and vice versa.
